# Get Started With a New Sample


You just received notification that uBiome has finished processing your sample. Now what do you do?


The first stop of course is the [uBiome web dashboard](http://app.ubiome.com), which you probably clicked on anyway from your email notification. The instructions there are self-explanatory and you'll learn most of what you need to know by simply clicking around. The following explanation will help you dig deeper.


If you only have one sample, your page will look something like this:

![](OverallSampleJun16.jpg)


This colorful bar chart is your first glimpse of the overall sample. Hover over it with your mouse (or tap with your finger if you're on a phone or iPad), to see a pop-up view of the approximate percentages.


You're seeing a *phyla*-based look at your microbiome. This is a very high level view, a bit like how a Martian might look at life on earth from far in outer space.  Keep in mind, by "very high level", I mean *very* high: the equivalent of seeing a breakdown of all animals in big categories like "vertebrate" and "invertebrate". You don't even bother separating fish from one another or for that matter, from land animals: just lump all of them  together. 


At the phyla level, you can't even tell, say, mammals from reptiles -- they're all the same.


, where you lump together everything from worms and fish to people and birds.

## Check your key ratios


Like every ecology, your microbes are all inter-related. Too much or too little of one or another and things go haywire. It's all about balance. For that reason, rather than focus on absolute counts, it's more helpful to look at the ratios of some key organisms.
